✅ Prevention Tips

1. Use strong, unique passwords for each account and enable 2-factor authentication (2FA).
2. Never share your OTP, passwords, or UPI PIN with anyone—even if they claim to be from a bank.
3. Double-check UPI requests – remember, entering your UPI PIN means you are sending money, not receiving it.
4. Avoid clicking on unknown links received via SMS, email, or social media.
5. Verify profiles and messages before trusting job offers, internships, or giveaways online.
6. Limit personal information shared on social media (phone number, address, college ID, etc.).
7. Download apps only from official sources like Google Play Store or Apple App Store.
8. Log out from shared/public devices and avoid saving passwords on them.
9. Be cautious on public Wi-Fi – avoid logging into banking or payment apps on unsecured networks.
10. Keep your devices updated with the latest security patches and antivirus software.

🚨 If Something Goes Wrong

Report immediately on: https://cybercrime.gov.in

Call the Cyber Crime Helpline: 1930 (for quick action in financial fraud cases)
